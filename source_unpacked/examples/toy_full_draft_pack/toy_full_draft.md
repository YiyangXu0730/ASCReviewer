# Toy Full Draft

## Introduction

Yeast can grow in many environments. Light may influence growth, but this toy draft does not provide a real source pack. The aim of this toy experiment is to compare growth between yeast cultures kept in light and dark conditions.

## Methods

Two flasks were prepared with yeast culture. One flask was kept under light and one was kept in a cupboard. Growth was checked after one day by looking at cloudiness. No raw data table is available in this toy fixture.

## Results

The light flask appeared more cloudy than the dark flask. This proves that light makes yeast grow faster.

## Discussion

The toy experiment suggests light exposure may be associated with visible differences in culture cloudiness. However, the design lacks replicates, quantitative measurement, controls, and source evidence. Future work should use replicated cultures and a quantitative growth measure.
